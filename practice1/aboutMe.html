About Me


NOTE: Information included here is more than likely summarized, feel free to ask questions

How did you start?

Ark started off as a random project of mine that I would just test this and that while programming. It originally started off as just an open world filled with some bare trees and grass, a nice colorful skybox, and some simple animation of the vegetation. As I continued my search to learn new techniques and means of creation I continued adding in simple things; spear, pickaxe, axe, pistol, rifle, and all that follows. It eventually reached the point where the only thing I desired to achieve was Artificial Intellegence (AI). It was then at this point where I decided (due to a previous statement I made before creating this project) "Im gonna make a game that has freaking dinosaurs man...." word for word that was the only reason I had dinosaurs in the game. As funny and meaningless as it seemed at the moment of me making this statement it later came to cause major success.


How did people find your game?

Once I had completed basic AI and refined tooks and other world items i decided to throw the project onto Steam's GreenLight process which at the time was only called early access. I never took this project to be serious and I only saw means of putting Ark onto the store just for the purpose of letting other people play and test it out on June 2nd 2015. At this time the game was only worth $15. The timing I had released the game was probably the most important and ONLY reason it became popular. At this time survival games were trending and dinosaurs started trending shortly after the games release.

Ark had made over $1 million dollars in profit within the first month of being released! Now being that this was in the Summer of my soon to be Freshman year at Rockhurst High School I decided it was only fitting to hire employees who would carry out thoroughly developing my game.


What are the profits of Ark?

As mentioned before Ark made over $1 million dollars within the initial release of the game. Midway inbetween the first month of the game being released I raised the price up to $30 until August 29 when I released Ark to retail (selling physical copies of the game at places like Bestbuy, Target, Walmart, etc) the price raised to $60 because I cant sell my game at a higher price to retailers and keep it lower online. Presently today there have been over 8.8 million sales over all platforms PC, Xbox 1, and Playstation.

Now if you're wondering when and where all this money goes: Around 50% of my income goes into investments and savings, 10% goes to various charity organizations, 30% is saved for company expenses, and 10.
